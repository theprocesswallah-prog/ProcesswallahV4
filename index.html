Processwallah was created after observing the same operational challenges repeatedly inside manufacturing companies:

  • Follow-up dependency — Daily delays caused by coordinators manually chasing supervisors for updates.
  • Reporting delays — Managing lines using trailing shift indicators instead of active floor records.
  • Process gaps — Critical specifications lost when transitioning orders from planning to operators.
  • Manual coordination — Relying on phone threads and verbal handshakes to align different shop sections.
  • Lack of visibility — No central reference point to trace a batch, drawing revision, or tooling change.
The Goal

Operational Upgrades

Before vs After Transformation

Transitioning your floor from chaotic manual follow-ups to systematic tracking and visibility.

Legacy Operation

  • WhatsApp follow-ups: Instructions and status updates get buried in personal threads and informal group chats.
  • Missed updates: Assembly lines stall because material delays or machine breakdowns are not immediately reported.
  • Manual tracking: Floor supervisors spend hours updating spreadsheets and physical logs at the end of every shift.
  • Delayed reporting: Decisions are made using historical daily performance summaries compiled days later.

Systematized Operation

  • Centralized workflow: Every active job card is visible on a structured interface accessible to all departments.
  • Live status visibility: Real-time production steps are tracked as they are cleared on the shop floor.
  • Automated tracking: Automated system transitions notify the next team when preceding parts clear quality checks.
  • Accountability trail: Every task, revision approval, and delay is logged with timestamps and owner tags.

Case Studies

Task Management System

A deep dive into how a centralized workflow platform replaced manual updates and improved operational efficiency.

The Problem

Departments and team leads were managing floor tasks, tooling changes, and maintenance requests through phone calls, meetings, and WhatsApp messages. This unmonitored communication led to delays, miscommunication, lack of clear accountability, and dispute management problems.

The Solution

We designed and implemented a centralized, web-based task management platform. Built to function on simple floor terminals, the tool tracks operational ownership, enforces specific due dates, records live progress logs, and organizes blueprint attachments directly on the active task cards.

The Business Outcome

Established a single, reliable source of truth across all shift operations. Managers have full visibility of active bottleneck states, preventing delayed orders and improving team coordination.
